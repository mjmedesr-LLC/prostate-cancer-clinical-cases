# Prostate Cancer Clinical Cases

Educational repository containing clinical case studies for prostate cancer management training.

## Project Purpose

This repository serves as a curated collection of clinical case studies focused on prostate cancer management. The goal is to provide healthcare professionals, trainees, and students with realistic, evidence-based clinical scenarios that illustrate the diagnostic workup, treatment decision-making, and follow-up considerations encountered in prostate cancer care. Each case is designed to promote critical thinking and reinforce best practices in oncology and urology.

## Target Audience

This repository is intended for:

- **Medical students** seeking to develop clinical reasoning skills in oncology and urology
- **Residents and fellows** in urology, radiation oncology, and medical oncology training programs
- **Practicing physicians** who wish to review and refresh their approach to prostate cancer cases
- **Nurse practitioners and physician assistants** involved in prostate cancer care
- **Allied health professionals** participating in multidisciplinary cancer care teams
- **Educators** looking for structured case-based teaching materials

## Case Structure

Each clinical case study in this repository follows a standardized format to ensure consistency and educational value:

1. **Case Title and Numbering** — Each case is assigned a sequential number and a descriptive title (e.g., `01-elevated-psa`)
2. **Patient History** — Demographics, presenting complaint, past medical history, family history, and social history
3. **Diagnostic Workup** — Laboratory findings, imaging results, pathology reports, and staging information
4. **Treatment Options** — Evidence-based treatment alternatives with rationale, including active surveillance, surgery, radiation therapy, and systemic therapies as applicable
5. **Discussion** — Key learning points, clinical pearls, decision-making rationale, and references to current guidelines (NCCN, EAU, AUA)

Cases are organized in individual directories with a `case_details.md` file as the primary document.

## Contribution Guidelines

We welcome contributions from the medical and educational community. To contribute:

1. **Fork the repository** and create a new branch for your case study
2. **Follow the case structure** outlined above to maintain consistency across the repository
3. **Ensure clinical accuracy** — All case content should reflect current evidence-based guidelines and standard of care. Include relevant citations where appropriate
4. **De-identify patient information** — All cases must be fully de-identified to protect patient privacy. Use fictionalized but clinically realistic scenarios
5. **Submit a pull request** with a clear description of the case you are adding or modifying
6. **Peer review** — All contributions will be reviewed for clinical accuracy, educational value, and adherence to the case structure before merging
7. **Report issues** — If you find errors or have suggestions for improvement, please open an issue with a detailed description

By contributing, you agree that your content will be licensed under the same terms as this repository and is intended solely for educational purposes.
